Description of mTCW overview

The mTCW database is created from two or more single TCW (sTCW) databases, where a sTCWdb may be composed on nucleotide (NT) or protein (AA) sequences. If this mTCW contains data from one or more AA-sTCWdbs, then many of the following statistics will not be present.

Most of the statistics in this overview can be computed from the clusters and sequence tables using the "Table..." Column Stats option, and from the pairs table using the "Show..." Column Stats option; the Column Stats also give the standard deviation, median and range. The column name is in the Column column.
LabelDescriptionColumn
DATASETS This first section describes the data imported from the sTCWdbs. The data is shown in the Sequences table.
Average Lengths The ORFs were computed for the sTCWdbs and imported along with their translated sequence. 5UTR Len, 3UTR Len, CDS Len
%GC The average percent of GC content per sequence. %GC
%CpG The average percent of CpG content per sequence. %CpG
Counts The total raw counts from each dataset. The RPKM values are in the sequence table, but not the counts. -
CLUSTER SETS One or more sets of clusters will be present in the mTCW. A given sequence can be in multiple cluster sets, but in a given cluster set only once. The data is shown in the Cluster table.
Sizes The number of sequences that are in clusters per cluster set. Count
Taxa categories An indicator of the composition of the cluster, where Mx1 means it contains M different datasets with exactly one member from each, while MxN means there are M different datasets, and one or more datasets has more than one sequence. Taxa
PAIRS Similar pairs are identified from the self-blast, where AA is from the amino acid search, and if NT exists, it is from the nucleotide search. The data is shown in the Pairs table.
Dataset Based on the blast output, count of pairs from two different datasets and the count of pairs from the same dataset.
The Pair Filter under "Dataset" lets you perform this query. 		-
Similarity Average percent similarity (3rd column of blast file, often referred to as %Identity). %AAsim, %NTsim
Overlap Average percent overlap of the alignment (4th column of blast file) to the total length of the sequence. There are two percent overlap values for each pair. %AAolap1/2, %NTolap1/2
Program Search program used, e.g. Blast, Diamond, Usearch. This is relevant as they give slightly different results. -
Aligned Pairs The pairs in clusters are aligned using dynamic programming and statistics computed.
Codons
Exact Average percent of #codons that are exactly the same. %Cexact
Synonymous Average percent of #codons that are synonymous (different codon, same amino acid). %Csyn
Fourfold Average percent of codons that are fourfold (4d), which is a synonymous codon where the ith position allows any of the 4 bases. %C4d
Twofold Average percent of codons that are fourfold (2d), which is a synonymous codon where the ith position allows any of the 2 bases. %C2d
Nonsynonymous Average percent of #codons that are nonsynonynous (different amino acid). %CnonSyn
Amino Acids
Exact Average percent of the #AA that are the same. %Aexact
Substitution>0 Average percent of #AA substitutions with BLOSUM62 score >0. %Apos
Substitution<=0 Average percent of #AA substitutions with BLOSUM62 score <=0. %Aneg
Nucleotides
CDS gap Average percent gaps of total aligned bases including gaps. The #Gaps is a column, but not the %Gap. -
CDS diff For the CDS alignments, average percent differences of total aligned bases including gaps. Cdiff
5UTR diff For the 5'UTR alignments, average percent differences of total aligned bases including gaps. 5diff
3UTR diff For the 3'UTR alignments, average percent differences of total aligned bases including gaps. 3diff
CDS base substitution Total number of SNPs for the whole dataset.
Transition Average percent of SNPs that are transitions in each of the three codon positions. -
Transversion Average percent of SNPs that are transversion in each of the three codon positions. -
ts/tv Average count of transitions divided by count of transversions. ts/tv
Content For the By Nucleotide, all CpGs occurrences were counted, even if they crossed codon boundaries.
For the By Codon, CpG occurrences were not counted if they crossed a codon boundary.
Both Average of the count of base alignments where both sequences have a G or C or CpG. -
Either Average of the count of base alignments where either (or both) sequences have a G or C or CpG. -
B/E Average value of 'Both' count divided by the 'Either' count (i.e. the Jaccard Index). There is a pair column for the GC and codon-based CpG, but not the nucleotide-base CpG. GC, CpG
KaKs method The KaKs_calculator (Zhang et al. 2006) is used to compute the KaKs values, where the method used is shown on the "KaKs Method" line.
KaKsSelective strength (<1 is purifying, >1 is positive, significantly >1 implies that at least some of the mutations are probably advantageous). KaKs
